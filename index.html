Bertha Lutz's Tribute Page

A woman ahead of her time

Bertha Lutz, in the center, speeaching in a UN meeting, surrounded by male scientists, in majority.

Bertha Maria Júlia Lutz was born in São Paulo, Brazil, in August 1894. Biologist by profession was the daughter of scientist and pioneer of Tropical Medicine Adolfo Lutz and of English nurse Amy Fowler. As a teenager, she came into contact with the explosive British suffrage campaign. She graduated in 1918 in Sciences (natural sciences) in Paris, at the Sorbonne, specializing in anuran amphibians.

  • 1919 - Bertha takes a public examination as a biologist at the National Museum, becoming the second Brazilian to enter the public service in Brazil, building an international reputation as a scientist.
  • 1919 - Represented Brazil, together with Olga de Paiva Meirano from São Paulo, the International Women's Council, in the body of the International Labor Organization (ILO), where the principles of equal pay for both sexes and the inclusion of women in the service to protect workers.
  • 1920'-30' - She was also the founder of the Brazilian Federation for Female Progress – FBPF, which led the suffrage campaign in the country. Throughout her years of intense militancy, Bertha tried to reconcile her political activity with her professional interest and always took advantage of her travels to carry out studies related to her specialty abroad. She also graduated in Law at the Faculty of Rio de Janeiro, which was later integrated into the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1920'-30' - Bertha and other FBPF women's activists submitted petitions of thousands of women seeking the woman's vote. Even appointed to a committee responsible for drafting the Electoral Code, she led three unsuccessful attempts.
  • 1932 - Getúlio Vargas, Brazilian president during dictatorship, signed the decree of the new Electoral Code, in which women in Brazil acquired the right to vote. Two years later, the Constitution reaffirms that right.
  • 1936 - Bertha Lutz assumed the mandate of federal deputy, presenting the bill of the Women's Statute, which reformed Brazilian legislation regarding the role of female work. Its main banners were changes related to women's right to work, against child labor, right to maternity leave and the equalization of their wages and rights.
  • 1975 - The United Nations establishes 1975 as the “International Year of Women”. The Brazilian government invited Bertha to join the Brazilian delegation in the organization's first International Women's Congress. It was her last act in favor of the female condition, as she was already sick.
  • 1976 - Bertha Lutz died in Rio de Janeiro on September 16, 1976.

    • In addition to her intense work as an activist in the feminist movement, she worked for four decades as a professor and researcher at the Museu Nacional, in Rio de Janeiro, being recognized worldwide for her contribution to zoological research on Brazilian amphibian species. She has published articles and scientific papers in both zoology and botany. She worked with her father, Adolpho Lutz, taking care of his collection of Amphibian Anurans and his first herbarium.
